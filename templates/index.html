About Us

Welcome to our Coffee Leaf Disease Classification project, "Barako - BAMA (Brewed Analysis for Monitoring Ailment)". We are a group of 3rd-year BS Computer Science students from FAITH Colleges.

Our project utilizes deep learning techniques with the ResNet50 model architecture to accurately classify various coffee leaf diseases. To ensure ease of access and usability, we have implemented our solution using the Flask framework, providing a user-friendly interface for every users.

LEAF CLASSIFICATION

Healthy

A healthy coffee leaf.

Leaf Rust

Circular orange-brown lesions. Leaf rust, also known as brown rust (Puccinia triticina Eriks.) is a major disease of durum wheat throughout the world. It is the most prevalent of all the wheat rust diseases, occurring in nearly all areas where wheat is grown. Leaf rust development is favored by moist and warm growing conditions.

Solution: Regularly spray copper-based fungicides.

Lichens

Green-gray/white patches on leaves. A lichen consists of a simple photosynthesizing organism, usually a green alga or cyanobacterium, surrounded by filaments of a fungus. Generally, most of a lichen's bulk is made of interwoven fungal filaments, but this is reversed in filamentous and gelatinous lichens. The fungus is called a mycobiont.

Solution: Regularly apply fungicides and improve air circulation.

Sooty Mold

Black, powdery fungal coating. Sooty mould is a fungus that feeds on the honeydew, covering the leaves and stems in a black sticky substance. It does not feed directly on the plant's foliage; however, it covers the plant's leaf surface, blocking light and reducing photosynthesis, essential for plant growth.

Solution: Regular cleaning and control insect pests.

Wilted Leaf

Wilted, limp, yellowed foliage. Abiotic factor. Plants wilt when roots are unable to supply sufficient moisture to the stems and leaves. Wilting for short periods of time does not harm plants. Sometimes a plant wilts on a hot day because moisture is evaporating from the leaves faster than the roots can take it up.

Solution: Ensure proper watering and maintain soil health.
